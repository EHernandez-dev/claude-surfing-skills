## Overview

La Salvaje (Barinatxe) is a **beach** break over **sand with rock/reef fingers** that breaks **both ways** off multiple peaks. The sandy banks suit **beginner to intermediate** surfers; the reef peaks are for **intermediate to advanced**. Best in **September-May** (summer is small and learner-friendly). Consistency: **dependable year-round, most consistent in winter** ([surf-forecast.com](https://www.surf-forecast.com/breaks/La-Salvaje) rates consistency 4/10 with December cleanest). The break is located at 43.3905, -3.0015 ([Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=43.3905,-3.0015) | [Windy waves](https://www.windy.com/-Waves-waves?waves,43.3905,-3.0015,10)).

A 752 m cliff-backed, clothing-optional beach between Sopela and Getxo with several named peaks: sandy mid-beach banks where the surf schools teach, and the rocky **La Triangular** reef peak that [The Surf Atlas](https://thesurfatlas.com/surfing-spain/sopelana-surf/) calls the best-quality wave on the whole Sopelana stretch. Today it is tiny summer surf: the report below is about the learner banks, not the reef.

**Works on:** NW swell, roughly 0.5-1.5 m for the sand banks (La Triangular needs 2.5 m+ and holds 4 m), S-SE offshore wind, all tides on the sand (sandbar A-frame best near high). *(Synthesized from spot guides; [Wannasurf](https://www.wannasurf.com/spot/Europe/Spain/Pais_Vasco/la_salvaje/) lists ideal swell "SE", which contradicts the NNW-facing geography and every other source - see Information Gaps.)*

## The Wave

A long, exposed beach break with shifting sandy peaks and rock/reef fingers at the named peaks. [Wannasurf](https://www.wannasurf.com/spot/Europe/Spain/Pais_Vasco/la_salvaje/) describes waves of normal length (50-150 m) working at all tide stages with falling tide the ideal movement, crowded lineups, and a rocky bottom at the reef sections. [The Surf Atlas](https://thesurfatlas.com/surfing-spain/sopelana-surf/) describes sandbar A-frames adjacent to La Triangular, best on high tide, with the reef sections offering better rights than lefts. In summer the beach is a learner zone: small NW windswell peaks over sand. In winter it is a serious beach break; [Wannasurf](https://www.wannasurf.com/spot/Europe/Spain/Pais_Vasco/la_salvaje/) reports La Triangular holding up to 4 m.

Sources conflict on ideal swell direction: [Wannasurf](https://www.wannasurf.com/spot/Europe/Spain/Pais_Vasco/la_salvaje/) lists SE, while [surf-forecast.com](https://www.surf-forecast.com/breaks/La-Salvaje) and geography say NW. The NNW-facing coastline makes NW the only physically sensible answer; the Wannasurf entry is treated as a data-entry error and flagged in Information Gaps.

## Hazards

**Rip currents:** The central beach has a named peak literally called La Corriente ("the current") and a buoyed stream outlet; expect channels and moving water along the 752 m beach even on small days. Paddle parallel to shore to escape; on a knee-high day like today the consequence is low but the habit matters.

**Rocks / reef:** Rock fingers and reef at La Triangular and the beach edges ([Wannasurf](https://www.wannasurf.com/spot/Europe/Spain/Pais_Vasco/la_salvaje/) lists a rocky bottom as the main hazard). Stay on the sandy mid-beach today; the reef ends are a non-issue at this size unless you drift onto them.

**Wave hazards:** None at today's size. In winter swell this becomes a powerful beach break (La Triangular holds 4 m); the character warning matters for return trips, not today.

**Crowds & localism:** [surf-forecast.com](https://www.surf-forecast.com/breaks/La-Salvaje) warns crowds "can reach hazard levels" when the surf is up and even suggests a helmet. Guide-level commentary describes a strong local crew with reputations ranging from "welcoming with respect" to "heavy" - **no dated first-hand account or documented incident was found**, so treat localism as unverified reputation (evidence level: low). On a summer Sunday expect a packed learner lineup around the schools rather than territorial locals.

**Cliff / access:** The beach is cliff-backed and the access ramp suffered structural collapses in early 2026; Bizkaia and Sopela authorities cleared and stabilized it for summer per [surf30.net (2026-05-15)](https://www.surf30.net/2026/05/la-rampa-de-la-salvaje-se-abrira-antes.html). Verify the ramp is open before committing to the walk; further closures are possible.

**Water quality:** See Current Conditions - clean per the July 2026 Basque Government classification, but avoid the buoyed central stream outlet, especially after today's likely rain.

**Skill-level match:** Today: anyone who can paddle, on the banks - it is a learner day. Generally: beginners and intermediates belong on the sand banks; La Triangular and winter days over ~1.5 m are for confident intermediates and up. For Elena (intermediate, comfort to ~1.5 m clean): everything on offer today and this week is inside the comfort zone; boredom is the bigger risk.
